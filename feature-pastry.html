Pastry of the Month

Kouign Amann

Pastry of the Month, Feb 26, 2017

Say hello to the Kouign Amann. Queen of my heart and the stuff your dreams will be made of after you taste a good one. Pronounced queen-ya-mahn, this little baby hails from Brittany, Franceâ€™s northwestern most region. It comes in all sorts of shapes and sizes and isn't always much of a looker but this one is all about the feel. Sweet and salty caramelized crunchy ewy-gewy bottom, rich dense buttery middle topped off with a flaky crusty top. Butter and sugar are layered throughout creating the rich texture and flavors.

I have two favorite spots to find these in the city, Le Reve in upper Queen Anne and Rosellini's in Ballard. Their versions are fairly different, Le Reve's a large soft ball sized muffin looking hunk of goodness as described above and Rosellini's a more refined small mini bundt-cake without the hole version, fully caramelized and sprinkled with sea salt flakes. Seek out both, neither will disappoint.
